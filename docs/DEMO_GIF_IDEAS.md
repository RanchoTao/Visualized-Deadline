# Demo GIF Ideas

VD needs demos that immediately communicate: this is not a normal todo app. Each GIF should show pressure, structure, or life context changing visually.

---

## 20 High-Impact Demo Ideas

### 1. Pressure Rising Over Time

Show three tasks with different deadlines. Advance the date or adjust deadlines so pressure visibly rises and the focus recommendation changes.

### 2. Completing a High-Pressure Task

Start in overload. Complete one urgent, important task. Show the pressure gauge dropping and the dashboard becoming calmer.

### 3. Recovery Activity Reduces Load

Add a recovery activity, mark it complete, and show recovery relief reducing overall pressure.

### 4. Importance Changes the Map

Edit a task from low importance to high importance. Show its visual weight and recommendation priority change immediately.

### 5. Deadline Collision

Create multiple tasks due on the same day. Show the interface revealing a pressure cluster instead of a simple chronological list.

### 6. Daily Control Center Flow

Open VD, review current pressure, inspect recommended focus, complete one task, and review the updated log.

### 7. First-Time Onboarding

Show quick task dumping, subjective pressure calibration, and the first generated pressure estimate.

### 8. Life Map Reveal

Zoom from the dashboard into the life map. Show “Me” connected to Academic, Research, Fitness, Finance, Social, Content, and Health.

### 9. Overloaded Life Domain

Add several academic tasks and show the Academic domain becoming visually heavier than other domains.

### 10. Social Graph Interaction

Open the social graph, select a person, edit relationship metadata, and show the node position or visual style update.

### 11. Neglected Relationship Signal

Simulate an old interaction date. Show a subtle relationship maintenance indicator appearing without guilt-heavy language.

### 12. Archive and Review

Complete a project, open the archive, expand it, and add a review note. Show that VD remembers the story after completion.

### 13. Abandoning Low-Value Work

Mark a low-value task as abandoned. Show pressure decreasing and an achievement unlocking for pruning unnecessary pressure.

### 14. Achievement Unlock

Complete the first task and show a tasteful achievement moment that reinforces momentum.

### 15. Backup Export

Open profile/data safety, export a backup, and show the generated `VD-backup-YYYY-MM-DD-HH-mm.json` file.

### 16. Backup Import Recovery

Import a backup and show tasks, profile, maps, and logs restored.

### 17. Shell Navigation

Move through Home, Map, Social, Logs, and Me in one fluid GIF to communicate VD as a shell, not a single page.

### 18. Pressure Explanation Tooltip

Hover or open a detail panel that explains why one task contributes more pressure than another.

### 19. Future AI Weekly Review Concept

Mock a future AI panel summarizing: “Your pressure is concentrated in Research and Health. Completing X would reduce load most.”

### 20. Future World Simulation Concept

Show a “what if” interaction: postpone a deadline, accept a new project, or add recovery time, then visualize future pressure changing.

---

## Visual Principles for GIFs

- Use realistic but anonymized life data.
- Make the pressure change obvious with color, scale, motion, or position.
- Avoid showing too many features in one GIF.
- Use short captions in the README table.
- Keep text large enough for GitHub mobile previews.
- Prefer a dark, futuristic look with calm motion.

## First Three GIFs to Produce

### GIF 1: “Pressure Is Not a Todo List”

Story:

1. Show a flat-looking task set.
2. Turn on pressure visualization.
3. One task becomes dominant because of urgency and importance.
4. User completes it.
5. Pressure drops.

### GIF 2: “Life Map Explains the Source”

Story:

1. Dashboard shows high pressure.
2. User opens life map.
3. Academic and Research domains glow.
4. User sees pressure concentration.

### GIF 3: “Social Context Matters”

Story:

1. User opens social graph.
2. A neglected relationship shows a subtle signal.
3. User adds a follow-up task.
4. The relationship node returns to a calmer state.

## Demo Data Ideas

Use example tasks like:

- Thesis draft due in 3 days.
- Rent payment due tomorrow.
- Gym recovery session today.
- Research meeting next week.
- Call family this weekend.
- Portfolio update due Friday.
- Medical appointment scheduling.
- Project proposal due in 10 days.

The demo should feel like a real life, not a sterile sample app.
